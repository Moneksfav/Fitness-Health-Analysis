# 🏋️‍♂️ Fitness & Health Analysis Portfolio Project

## Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive data analysis of fitness, workout, and health metrics across different demographic groups. Using a combination of **Microsoft Excel** and **Power BI**, the analysis uncovers how daily lifestyle choices—such as sleep quality, stress levels, and workout selection—directly impact physical performance, calorie burning, and overall energy levels.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
*   **Microsoft Excel:** Applied for robust data cleaning, handling missing values, and structuring the raw health data.
*   **Power BI:** Developed an interactive dashboard utilizing advanced data modeling and custom visualizations.
*   **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions):** Wrote custom DAX measures and calculated columns to generate dynamic Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and time-intelligence metrics.

---

## 📈 Key Insights & Business Findings

### 1. The Direct Impact of Stress on Physical Energy
High stress levels actively undermine daily physical activity. Data modeling reveals a strong inverse relationship between stress and mobility: on days when individuals reported maximum stress, their **total daily step counts dropped significantly**. Managing stress is fundamentally tied to maintaining physical stamina.

### 2. Yoga Emerges as the Most Efficient Calorie Burner
When evaluating different exercise regimens under the `Workout Type` attribute, **Yoga** surprisingly emerged as the fastest and most efficient routine for burning calories within the observed dataset, challenging the traditional assumption that high-impact cardio is the sole driver of calorie expenditure.

### 3. Demographic Activity Gap (Youth vs. Older Adults)
A clear age-based divergence exists in daily movement patterns. The **19–25 age cohort** consistently recorded the highest step counts globally, heavily outperforming the **51–56 age group**, highlighting where fitness engagement and health-promotion campaigns need to be targeted most.

### 4. Gender-Driven Workout Preferences
The interactive dashboard highlights how gender influences both overall mobility and specific fitness choices. It breaks down distinct patterns in daily step count averages between male and female participants, alongside identifying which specific workout types are heavily favored by each gender.
